Transport Canada (TC) Environmental Services — Paciﬁc Region has reviewed the South Peace
Pipeline Application (February 27, 2008) submitted by the proponent to Transport Canada. The
resulting comments herein are based upon Transport Canada's mandate, roles and
responsibilities.
TC Involvement
Transport Canada has identiﬁed itself as a Responsible Authority for this project, pursuant to
Section 5 of the Canadian EnvironmentafAssessmentAct (CEAA), on the basis that the route
proposed by the proponent will be passing through, in, ever, or under multiple watereeurses that
may require authorizations pursuant to Section 5(1) of the Navigable Waters Pmtectfon Act
andlor section 108 of the National Energy Board Act (Law List triggers).
The prepenent should submit application(s) to the NWP Program (contact information below) for
the following crossings deemed navigable under the Navigable Waters Protection Act
Kietkatinaw South, Kiskatinaw North, Brassey Creek and Oetata Creek; as well as any other
proposed new or upgraded bridge crossings, as seen as possible, to avoid undue delays.

environmental effects likely to result from issuing these approvals. including the effects of any
change on the environment on the current use of lands and resources for traditional purposes by

important to understand the nature of any such current or traditional activities. As such, Transport
Canada will be contacting First Nations to obtain this information. Any information that is received
from First Nations will be forwarded to the National Energy Board for their review.
